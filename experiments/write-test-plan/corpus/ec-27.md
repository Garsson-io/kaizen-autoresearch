A delivery route optimization service accepts a list of addresses, converts them to coordinates using a geocoding API, computes the shortest route using a traveling-salesman heuristic, estimates delivery time windows based on distance and configurable average speed, and validates that the total route fits within the driver's shift hours.

## Behaviors to cover

1. The traveling-salesman heuristic produces a route that is no more than 1.5x the length of the optimal route for inputs up to 20 stops.
2. The delivery time window estimates are computed correctly from the distance between consecutive stops and the configured average speed for the road type.
3. The shift-hours validator rejects a route whose total estimated time exceeds the driver's remaining shift hours, returning the excess amount.
4. When one address in the list fails geocoding (API returns no results), the service skips that stop and optimizes the remaining addresses, reporting which addresses were skipped.
5. Duplicate addresses in the input list are consolidated into a single stop with combined delivery items, not visited twice.
