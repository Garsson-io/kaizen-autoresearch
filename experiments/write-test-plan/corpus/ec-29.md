A patient intake system for a clinic collects patient information, validates insurance eligibility, performs AI-assisted symptom triage to suggest urgency level, schedules an appointment, and sends a confirmation. The insurance check calls an external payer API. The symptom triage asks an AI medical reasoning engine to assess urgency based on reported symptoms. Scheduling writes to a shared calendar database.

## Behaviors to cover

1. Required patient fields (name, date of birth, insurance ID) are validated before any downstream processing — missing fields produce a clear error listing all missing items.
2. Date of birth validation rejects future dates, dates more than 150 years ago, and non-date strings.
3. The insurance eligibility check sends the correct patient ID and plan code to the payer API and correctly interprets the "eligible" vs "not eligible" vs "pending" response.
4. When the payer API is unreachable, the system marks eligibility as "pending" and continues the intake rather than blocking.
5. The AI symptom triage correctly assigns high urgency to symptom combinations that indicate emergency conditions (e.g., chest pain + shortness of breath).
6. The symptom triage produces a natural language explanation of its urgency assessment that a clinician can review, not just a numeric score.
7. An appointment is scheduled in the first available slot that matches the assigned urgency level — high urgency gets same-day, medium gets within 48 hours.
8. The scheduling step does not double-book a time slot even when two patients are being processed concurrently.
9. The confirmation message includes the patient name, appointment time, provider name, and preparation instructions appropriate to the visit type.
10. The full intake pipeline — validate, check insurance, triage symptoms, schedule, confirm — completes for a real patient submission without manual intervention.
