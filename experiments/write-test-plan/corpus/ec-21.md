A customer feedback processing service receives text reviews and produces two outputs: a sentiment score (positive/negative/neutral) and a detailed explanation of the sentiment drivers. The sentiment score is computed by a keyword-frequency analyzer with configurable thresholds. The explanation is generated by a separate analyzer that interprets the review in context and produces natural language reasoning about why the customer feels the way they do.

## Behaviors to cover

1. The keyword-frequency analyzer assigns the correct sentiment when the review contains only words from the configured positive and negative word lists.
2. The explanation analyzer produces reasoning that references specific phrases from the original review, not generic boilerplate.
3. When a review contains mixed signals (both positive and negative keywords), the sentiment score reflects the configured tie-breaking rule.
4. The explanation for a sarcastic review (e.g., "Great, another broken feature") correctly identifies the negative intent despite positive surface words.
5. Both outputs (score and explanation) are returned together in a single response, with the explanation referencing the same sentiment direction as the score.
