An automated content moderation service receives user-submitted images and text posts. It uses an AI vision model to classify images and an AI text model to classify text. Posts flagged by either model are held for human review. The system must handle high throughput during peak hours.

## Behaviors to cover

1. An image containing explicit content is flagged and held for review, not published.
2. A text post containing hate speech is flagged regardless of obfuscation techniques (e.g., letter substitution, unicode lookalikes).
3. A post that passes both content checks is published rather than held or silently dropped.
4. The full moderation pipeline — image check, text check, publish decision — completes within 5 seconds of submission.
5. When the AI vision model is unavailable, image posts are queued for retry rather than auto-approved.
6. The moderation decision for a borderline image is consistent when the same image is submitted multiple times within a short window.
