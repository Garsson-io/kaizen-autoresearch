A meeting transcription service receives an audio file, sends it to a speech-to-text API, then passes the transcript to an LLM to produce a structured summary with action items, key decisions, and attendee attributions.

## Behaviors to cover

1. The speech-to-text output preserves speaker labels so that the summary can attribute statements to specific attendees.
2. The structured summary contains at least one action item for every attendee who was assigned a task during the meeting.
3. Key decisions mentioned in the transcript appear in the "decisions" section of the summary, not buried in the narrative.
4. When the audio file is longer than 60 minutes, the service splits it into segments and processes them sequentially, merging the results into a single coherent summary.
5. The full pipeline — upload audio, transcribe, summarize, deliver structured output — completes without manual intervention for a real meeting recording.
