A document analysis system processes legal contracts by running three independent AI reviewers in parallel: a clause extractor that identifies key terms, a risk assessor that flags problematic language, and a compliance checker that verifies regulatory requirements. Each reviewer operates independently on the same document. A merge step combines the three outputs into a unified report, resolving conflicts when reviewers disagree about the same clause.

## Behaviors to cover

1. Reviewer dispatch sends the same source document and correct reviewer-specific instruction pack to each reviewer role.
2. The live clause extractor identifies key terms (parties, dates, obligations, penalties) present in the contract.
3. Risk-policy configuration is passed correctly to the risk assessor invocation.
4. The live risk assessor flags language that is problematic under policy, including indirect or euphemistic phrasing.
5. Jurisdiction and regulatory profile are passed correctly to the compliance checker invocation.
6. The live compliance checker correctly determines whether the contract meets all configured regulatory requirements.
7. When two reviewers flag the same clause with conflicting assessments (for example extractor says `standard` but risk assessor says `high risk`), the merge step preserves both assessments with attribution.
8. End-to-end orchestration (dispatch all three reviewers, collect outputs, merge unified report) succeeds on a real contract without losing any reviewer findings.
