A document analysis system processes legal contracts by running three independent AI reviewers in parallel: a clause extractor that identifies key terms, a risk assessor that flags problematic language, and a compliance checker that verifies regulatory requirements. Each reviewer operates independently on the same document. A merge step combines the three outputs into a unified report, resolving conflicts when reviewers disagree about the same clause.

## Behaviors to cover

1. The clause extractor identifies all key terms (parties, dates, obligations, penalties) present in the contract.
2. The risk assessor flags language that would be problematic under the configured risk policy, even when the problematic phrasing is indirect or euphemistic.
3. The compliance checker correctly determines whether the contract meets all applicable regulatory requirements for the configured jurisdiction.
4. When two reviewers flag the same clause with conflicting assessments (e.g., extractor says "standard" but risk assessor says "high risk"), the merge step preserves both assessments with attribution.
5. The full pipeline — dispatching to all three reviewers, collecting results, merging into a unified report — completes correctly for a real contract without losing any individual reviewer's findings.
